EASY SUB

EASY SUB

EasySub is an Android application designed to connect university students who are looking for a sublet during their co-op terms. It does so by using an intuitive interface to link students needing to sublet their properties, to studnets looking to find a property to sublet.

Subletters can view available properties in the Kitchener/Waterloo region, read the property details, and contact the sublettees of the properties that interest them. On the other hand, sublettees can post descriptive details about their property, including pictures, price, and amenities to entice the many subletters browsing on EasySub.

The EasySub app was designed as a Hack the North project, and was therefore built in a short 36 hours! It was programmed in Java using Android Studio. A Google Maps API was also implemented in order to help users view the available properties around them on a map.

IoT GOOSEBOT

IoT GOOSEBOT

Have you ever seen one of those videos of a box that simply turns itself off after being turned on by a user and thought, "...How useless?" Well I have found a way to make that box just a little less... useless! The University of Waterloo's 2016 EngHack Winner, was awarded to my two teammates and I, who designed a Waterloo themed useless box that uses an Android app to incorporate user customization. This not only added awesome IoT functionality to the box, but additionally gives the user the power to remotely define new patterns in which the box can turn itself off - via an array of light bulbs, speakers, and servo motors.

My team members and I started this project by building a wooden box at the machine shop that enclosed all the necessary hardware components. Next we wired, soldered, and assembled all of our circuit elements with an Arduino Uno driving the system. Finally, we developed the control program of the GooseBot that was written in C++ in such a way that allows the user's box to open in one of two ways: with preset patterns or patterns designed by the user on the Android app. Check out the code here!

HOMEMADE CALIPER

CALIPER

Developed a caliper with 3 classmates that has the ability to measure lengths between 0 mm to 120 mm with a calculated maximum uncertainty of 11 mm. The caliper uses a rack and pinion to convert linear displacment into rotary motion, a potentiometer to convert the angular displacment of a gear to units of voltage, and a LCD to display the measurements to the user.

I lead the design of the rack and pinion that was laser cut from a sheet of 5mm thick transparent plastic. I designed the rack and pinion in AutoCAD to have an appropriate mesh when actuated.

CANTILEVER BEAM

CANTILEVER BEAM

The problem being tackled in this project is the creation of a three dimensional, cantilever beam truss with the highest load to mass ratio possible, by utilizing concepts developed in Mechatronics Engineering courses: Mechanics of Deformable Solids, Statics, and Structure and Property of Materials. The structure was designed with the constraints of using only a limited amount of balsa wood and dowel, as well as the structure fitting within predefined dimensions.

The load to mass ratio of the cantilever beam is determined by first measuring the mass of the structure, and then loading the cantilever beam in 500g increments until failure. Upon failure, the load to mass ratio is calculated as the quotient of the total mass that could be held and the mass of the truss. A beam was designed by two team members and I that minimized tension and maximized compression memebers in order to optimize the design and achieve the highest load to mass ratio.

The final truss was modeled and optimized in both Solidworks and AutoCAD. It had a weight of approximately 16.3 g and held a weight of 2.5 kg before failure. This resulted in a performance value of 153.

HUMAN GRASP ROBOT

HUMAN GRASP ROBOT

The Hand Grasp Robot was created with the goal of building a mechatronic system that simulates the grasping action of the human hand around everyday objects. In order to do so, the robot was designed to simulate power and precision gripping; the two most common grasps the human hand uses to hold or suspend objects. The robotic hand can help people who struggle with disabilities that impede their ability to grasp by reducing the hindrance of performing daily tasks that require these two grasping skills.

The robot was made with a LEGO NXT Platform and programmed in RobotC language. It was created with three appendages that modeled two fingers and a thumb. Each of these were built with rotating joints and string to represent tendons and joints of the human hand, allowing the robot to move in a human like manor. The final robot was able to suspend a full water bottle with a power grasp and a pencil with a precision grasp.
